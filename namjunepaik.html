Nam June Paik

Nam June Paik, a key figure in the experimental Fluxus movement, is acknowledged as the father of video art. With astonishing insight, Paik's practice—which fused music, performance, technology, and film—examined the potential of mass media and developing technologies. His most well-known works incorporate actual TVs, arranging them into robot shapes, stacking them in intimidating walls, placing them among lush plants in dimly lit galleries, and manipulating them to display distorted vision.

Takeshi Murata

Takeshi Murata is best recognized for his trippy animated videos. He is considered a pioneer of datamoshing, a genre of glitch art that manipulates compressed frames to give them an exceedingly pixelated appearance. More recently, Murata has developed digital still lifes that incorporate numerous items including beer bottles, lemons, and VHS tapes with outdated technology debris and emblems. Essentially, Murata updates Dutch vanitas paintings for the digital age by using CGI to produce these objects that appear to be three-dimensional. In these pieces as well as in videos like I, Popeye (2010), Murata analyses the creation of pictures and their existence in the digital age.

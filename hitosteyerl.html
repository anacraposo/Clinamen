Hito Steyerl

Hito Steyerl analyzes the condition of the image in a world that has become more high-tech and globalized through her writing, videos, and performative lectures. In a tradition that also includes Chris Marker and Harun Farocki, Steyerl's work at the Venice Biennale combines elements of the documentary and the cinema essay to examine issues such as globalization, political economy, visual culture, and the state of art creation. The proliferation of images and how they imprint and impact our society on both a macro and micro scale is a constant motif in the work of the Berlin-based artist. According to Steyerl, "Images do not depict reality; rather, they create reality and are ingrained in us."
